Why Singapore's curve stands up straight

Over 1992–2025 unemployment stayed inside a narrow 1.4%–4.0% band while inflation ranged from −0.5% to 6.6%. The result is a near-vertical data cloud rather than the textbook downward slope. Two structural reasons: a tight labour market with a very low natural rate of unemployment, and a monetary regime in which the Monetary Authority of Singapore manages the exchange rate to anchor imported inflation — rather than steering domestic demand up and down a Phillips trade-off. Inflation here is driven far more by global energy, food and import prices (2008, 2011–12, 2022) than by the slack in Singapore's own labour market.

Methodology. Inflation is the year-on-year change in the annual-average Consumer Price Index (Singapore Department of Statistics, base 2024=100), computed in your browser from the raw index. Unemployment is the Ministry of Manpower Overall Unemployment Rate, Annual (annual average). The OLS trend regresses inflation on unemployment for the selected set of years; slope, Pearson r and R² are recomputed live.
